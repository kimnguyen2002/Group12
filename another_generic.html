Craps

• One of the most "fair" games to play at a casino is Craps. Here is one version of how to play:
• Roll a pair of fair six-sided dice.
• If you roll a 7 or 11, you win!
• If you roll a 2, 3, or 12, you lose.
• Otherwise, record what you've rolled. Let this sum be k; also known as your point.
• If you rolled a point, continue rolling the pair of dice until you get either your point (k) or a sum of seven on the two dice.
• If k comes up first, you win!
• If 7 comes up first, you lose.

Arup's Game of Dice

• Amazingly, this game is even more "fair" than Craps, but the house still has a 50.2% chance of winning, which is why the casino hasn't gone broke yet! Here are the rules:
• Roll a pair of dice.
• If you roll a sum of 11 or 12, you win.
• If you roll a sum of 2, you lose.
• Otherwise, record what you've rolled. Let this sum be k; also known as your point.
• Roll one more time. If this roll exceeds your point(k), you win!
• If this roll is the same as your point(k) or lower, you lose.
